Voice patch panels are physical connection devices in communication equipment rooms used for centralized management of telephone/audio lines. They primarily handle functions such as internal and external line patching, security unit access, patch cord scheduling, and fault isolation. They are often used in conjunction with MDF (Main Distribution Frame), DDF (Digital Distribution Frame), and ODF (Optical Distribution Frame) to form a comprehensive cabling system, widely applied in enterprise communications, telecommunications central offices, and data center voice subsystems.

OMDF (Optical Fiber Main Distribution Frame)

OMDF is designed to overcome the shortcomings of ODF (Optical Fiber Main Distribution Frame), such as small capacity, limited functionality, and inconvenient cable management. OMDF offers diverse functions.

The OMDF should be installed as close as possible to the OLT (Optical Line Terminal) or other equipment room, taking into account factors such as the routing and capacity of physical channels like outgoing conduits, building shafts, and cable trays. Installation can be on the same floor (or in the same equipment room) or on a different floor. The following priority order should be followed for specific installation:

  1. If the existing cable distribution room has sufficient space, prioritize installing the OMDF in the cable measurement room;
  2. If the existing cable distribution room does not have sufficient space, the OMDF should ideally be installed in the same equipment room as the OLT;
  3. If neither the existing OLT nor the transmission equipment room has sufficient space, a separate OMDF equipment room can be installed on the same floor or on a different floor.

Underground Fiber optic connectors

Underground fiber optic junction boxes overcome the various problems associated with traditional junction boxes, effectively solving construction and installation issues such as difficulties in land acquisition, municipal approval, impact on urban appearance, and susceptibility to theft and damage.

Furthermore, because they are installed and stored underground, compared to traditional junction boxes where temperature variations are minimal throughout the day and seasons and there is almost no solar radiation,

the lifespan of the fiber optic cables, connectors, and adapters inside the box is significantly increased, providing a more stable and secure guarantee for the local fiber optic network.
