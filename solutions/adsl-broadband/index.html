Advantages of ADSL broadband

ADSL (Asymmetric Digital Subscriber Line) broadband offers high-speed internet using existing copper telephone lines, providing a reliable, “always-on” connection without interrupting phone calls.

They transmit data directly through physical cables, are unaffected by electromagnetic interference, and are particularly suitable for enterprises, data centers, and fixed environments with high network quality requirements.

Our Process

Environmental Survey

Inspect the quality of the incoming line and determine the installation locations for the splitter and modem.

Splitter Installation

Connect the splitter to the incoming line to separate voice and data signals.

Network Cable Laying

Lay the network cable along the wall to the computer or router location and secure the line clips.

Equipment Connection

Connect the modem to the splitter, power supply, and router or computer.

Configure Parameters

Set VPI/VCI, username, and password to complete the dial-up connection.

Testing and Acceptance

Test the speed to verify that the bandwidth meets the requirements. Deliver the device after confirming stable internet access.
